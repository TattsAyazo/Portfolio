Fuego Con

What sparks creativity? Our creative studio had an inner creativity conference lead by the designers of our team. With the thought in mind that creativity is a spark of fire that is ignited within all of us, we wanted to portrait that with all the fire power of what it feels when your creativity is being lighted up. Flames, lighters, matches and creative dinamite it what feels like when you are giving birth to that creative idea.

Psychotropical Roots

Colombian and proud to be. Psychotropical roots is a personal project impregnated with the smells of a tropical jungle, the soul of Colombian ethnic tribes and my own personal empowerment of the rooted background of where I come from. Playing with color, textures and prints wanted to capture the bigger essence of these cultures. Highlight the cultures that came before up and enhance their traditions with color and joyfulness of remembering.

Don't Lie

Personal collage work for a set of postcards. Having an indigenous background it has been very rooted within me to collage and rescue part of my culture that has been lost and neglected in our country. This was an ode to a part of me that not a lot of people know about and wanted to portrait indigenous cultures as the tropical rooted force behind many of us.

Recognition Cards

Google's mastery offsite team wanted to recognize and highlight people in the team through Mastery's Values:

  1. Three Respects (Snow leopard)
  2. Empathy (Stag)
  3. Impact (Moth)
  4. Everyday Creativity (Dragon)

The brief was to create something Magical and eerie. One of the key themes we wanted to get across is we sometimes conflate "awards" and "recognition", yet we can recognize those that matter to us every day. Recognition from a peer is a magic in off itself. The idea was to highlight those who have meant something to us and to Mastery.

Recipe Book

This set of illustrations for my grandmother's cook book was an incredibly special experience for me. It allowed me to honor the woman who had such a profound impact on my life. Each illustration comes from grandmother's favorite recipes, as a tribute to the woman who taught me how to cook with love and soul. My hope was that these illustrations not only capture the essence of my grandmother's cooking style, but also serve as a reminder of the power of food to bring people together and create lasting memories.
